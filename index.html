The night air was bitterly cold, slicing into my very soul. It was, in a strange way, a welcome feeling. It was also slightly more bearable among my bare feet pressed firmly to the beach's shifting sands. A chilly winter breeze whirled off the glistening water, pulling my gaze away from the distant horizon.
It was so easy to get lost in thought amidst this incredibly vague, surreal environment. I felt like I had gone through some sort of portal - into a land of reflection and inner truth. Something about this place was oddly calming, and for the first time in a long time, I let my defenses down and allowed myself to not only feel, but to think, decipher and determine why I was feeling vulnerable in this world.
The beach had become my sanctuary. The ground beneath my feet felt oddly comforting with the softly-rolling grains of sand. It was just what I needed, if only for a brief moment. The ocean roared gracefully and I leaned forward onto my toes, savoring the release as the temperature of the sand embraced my feet. I felt safe and liberated, but I also knew this was a transient situation.
I wondered what else the sand was revealing to me. What wisdom does the shifting sand hold?
Digging deeper into my musings, I realized I felt somewhat ashamed of the person I had become after spending most of my life trying to make up for lost times, trying to find strength in things that could not be achieved fruitfully.
Yes, I had worked hard and made some progress, but I had to bring myself back down to the ground and confront reality before I could truly know my boundaries. The foundation of my life and happiness had been nothing more than shifting sands. How much was I declined, neglected, or lost out in life? How much potential had I postponed?
These were the questions I now faced on the beach.
The sands beneath my toes humbled me as I realized that I had more personal equity and control over my future than I thought. Despite the lack of power in life, shifting my mental sandbanks would help me access courage and direction in it. It showed me I had the power to look at myself, my mistakes, my fears, and still strive for better.
